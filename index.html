About Me

Hello,

I'm Tammy and I am a self-taught programming “student”. I started teaching myself to code in early 2017 after a long while of being interested, but never making the jump. I began with Java working through Codecademy & Udacity and worked on a few small projects before switching to HTML, CSS & Javascript. I've recently begun learning things such as Command Line/Vim, Git and Test Driven Development. I was recently accepted to the London developer training programme, Founders & Coders, for their Spring 2018 cohort beginning in February. I'm very excited about this opportunity and am looking forward to developing my skills by working and learning with others.

I graduated from California State University, Fresno with a Bachelor's degree in Communicative Disorders and Deaf Studies. I worked as a Sign Language Interpreter for 3 years, working with the Deaf, and occasionally Deaf-Blind, communities. During that time, I learned and experienced the importance of accessibility and how these communities often feel overlooked and ignored. I hope that I can bring my experiences into my future work by being an advocate for groups that are sometimes forgotten. Building this webpage provided me with an awesome opportunity to learn more about accessibility practices. I utilized ARIA label and tabindex to make all of my elements accessible by screen readers, testing it with ChromeVox and a11y.css. I appreciate the things I've learned so far and look forward to making content that is accessible to all.

This gallery is a place for me to display some of the photos I've taken over the last few years that I'm proud of. Please browse, click and hover to learn more about me and the photos you see on this page. You can also refresh to see different images. Enjoy!
