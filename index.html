ABOUT ME

HELLO,

I'm Tammy and I am a self-taught programming “student”. I started on this path in early 2017 after a long while of being interested in learning to code, but never making the jump. At this point, I have experience working with Javascript, Java and HTML/CSS. I'm applying for the developer training programme Founders & Coders in London because I'm interested in gaining skills to become a Full Stack Developer and I believe that Founders & Coders will help me get there. I have been learning mostly on my own and while I have been able to achieve quite a bit, I feel that being surrounded by other students with other strengths and other ways of approaching problems would benefit me greatly and allow for better and quicker growth.

I graduated from California State University, Fresno with a Bachelor's degree in Communicative Disorders and Deaf Studies. I worked as a Sign Language Interpreter for 3 years, working with the Deaf, and occasionally Deaf-Blind, communities. During that time, I learned and experienced the importance of accessibility and how these communities often feel overlooked and ignored. I believe I can bring my experiences into my future work by being an advocate for groups that are sometimes forgotten. Building this webpage provided me with an awesome opportunity to learn more about accessibility practices. I utilized ARIA label and tabindex to make all of my elements accessible by screen readers, testing it with ChromeVox and a11y.css. I appreciate the things I've learned so far and look forward to making content that is accessible to all.

This gallery is a place for me to display some of the photos I've taken over the last few years that I'm proud of. Please browse, click and hover to learn more about me and the photos you see on this page. You can also refresh to see different images. Enjoy!
